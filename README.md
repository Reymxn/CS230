CS-230 Module Eight Journal – Reflection
1. Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements.

The client for this project was The Gaming Room, a company that wanted to expand their existing game, Draw It or Lose It, into a web-based application that could support multiple teams and players across different platforms. Their primary requirement was a software design that allowed scalability, uniqueness of game instances, and cross-platform compatibility. The system needed to support multiple users simultaneously while maintaining consistent performance and reliability. The design focused on creating a structured architecture that would allow future growth and efficient game management.

2. What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?

One area I performed well in was organizing the software design document clearly and logically. I focused on explaining design decisions using professional terminology and ensuring that the architecture, requirements, and constraints were easy to understand. I also made sure that the documentation connected technical choices to client needs, which helped demonstrate how the design supported real-world business goals.

3. What about the process of working through a design document was helpful when developing the code?

Working through a design document helped establish a clear roadmap before coding began. By defining system requirements, constraints, and architecture early, I was able to avoid confusion later in development. The design document acted as a reference point that guided implementation decisions, reduced mistakes, and made it easier to maintain consistency throughout the development process.

4. If you could choose one part of your work to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

If I could revise one part of the documentation, I would improve the level of detail in the system architecture diagrams and technical explanations. Adding more visual diagrams and clearer relationships between components would make the design easier for both technical and non-technical stakeholders to understand. I would also include more specific examples to clarify how certain design patterns are applied.

5. How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

I interpreted the user’s needs by focusing on scalability, usability, and reliability. The design emphasized a system that could handle multiple users while maintaining consistent performance. Considering user needs is essential because software should solve real problems for the client and provide a positive experience for end users. Ignoring user requirements can lead to systems that are technically functional but fail to meet business or usability expectations.

6. How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future?

My approach involved analyzing requirements first, then breaking the system into smaller components using object-oriented design principles. I focused on modularity, clear separation of responsibilities, and maintainability. In the future, I would continue using structured design documents, UML diagrams, and iterative planning to improve efficiency. I would also involve earlier feedback loops to validate design choices before implementation.
